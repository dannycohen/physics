## Energy is a triangle

The equation looks like the Pythagorean theorem because it is one. Rest energy
`mc²` and the momentum term `pc` are the two perpendicular legs of a right
triangle, and the total energy `E` is the hypotenuse. That single picture holds
the whole idea.

Two corners of the triangle are famous in their own right. Stop the object dead:
`pc` drops to zero, the triangle collapses onto its upright leg, and `E = mc²` —
the equation everyone has seen on a T-shirt. Now take a particle of light, which
has no mass: the upright leg vanishes and `E = pc`, pure energy of motion. Every
real massive particle lives somewhere between those two corners, and the slider
walks it from one toward the other.

## The same γ again

Written out, the total energy is `E = γmc²` and the momentum term is `pc =
γβmc²`, both carrying the same Lorentz factor that stretches time and shrinks
length. As the speed climbs, γ climbs, the triangle grows, and reaching `c`
would demand infinite energy. See γ by itself on the{' '}
<a href={`${import.meta.env.BASE_URL}viz/foundations/lorentz-factor/`}>Lorentz factor page</a>.

<details class="misconception">
  <summary>Isn't E = mc² the energy of a moving object?</summary>

  No. `E = mc²` is only the *rest* energy — the value when the object is not
  moving and `pc` is zero. A moving object carries more: `E = γmc²`, which is
  always larger than `mc²`. The extra part, `(γ − 1)mc²`, is its kinetic energy.
  So `E = mc²` is not the whole story of energy, just the special corner of the
  triangle where the momentum leg has shrunk to nothing.
</details>
